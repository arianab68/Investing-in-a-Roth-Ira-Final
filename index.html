ABOUT ROTH IRA'S

What is a Roth Ira?

Saving for Retirement

A Roth IRA is a special retirement account where you pay taxes on money going into your account, and then all future withdrawals are tax-free.

Beat High Taxes

Roth IRAs are best when you think your taxes will be higher in retirement than they are right now.

Contributing

You can't contribute to a Roth IRA if you make too much money. In 2020, the limit for singles is $139,000. For married couples, the limit is $206,000

Availability

Almost all brokerage ﬁrms, both physical and online, offer a Roth IRA. So do most banks and investment companies.

STARTING A ROTH IRA.

A Roth IRA must be established with an institution that has received IRS approval to offer IRAs. These include banks, brokerage companies, federally insured credit unions, and savings and loan associations.

A Roth IRA can be established at any time. But contributions for a tax year must be made by the tax-filing deadline, which is usually on April 15th.

There are two basic documents that must be provided to the IRA owner when an IRA is established:

The IRA disclosure statement and the IRA adoption agreement. These provide an explanation of the rules and regulations under which the Roth IRA must abide by.These documents also establish an agreement between the IRA owner and the IRA trustee. HOW IT WORKS

Contributing to a Roth Ira.

What types of contributions are accepted?

All regular Roth IRA contributions must be made in cash including checks and they can't be in the form of securities or assets.

How much can I contribute?

$6,000 is the maximum annual contribution an individual can make in 2019 and 2020 to a Roth IRA but those 50 years old and up can contribute up to $7,000.

Are there any age restrictions or limits?

Contributions can continue to be made at any age, as long as the Roth Ira owner has earned income. The account holder can maintain the Roth IRA for as ling as they wish and there are no required minimum distributions.

Does my Roth Ira get taxed?

The money invested within the Roth IRA grows tax-free. However, the money you deposit would already be taxed.
